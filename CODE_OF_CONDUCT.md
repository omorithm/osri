# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to making participation in OSRI a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful experience for everyone.

We value contributions from people of all backgrounds and experience levels. We believe that diverse perspectives strengthen open-source communities and lead to better software.

---

## Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate toward others
- Communicate openly and constructively
- Welcome different perspectives and experiences
- Provide feedback focused on ideas and contributions
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what is best for the project and community

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation
- Personal attacks or hostile communication
- Trolling or intentionally disruptive behavior
- Publishing private information without permission
- Unwelcome sexual attention or inappropriate conduct
- Any behavior that creates an unsafe or unwelcoming environment

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior.

Maintainers may take appropriate action in response to behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, including:

- Warning the contributor
- Removing inappropriate comments or contributions
- Temporarily restricting participation
- Removing access to project spaces when necessary

---

## Reporting Issues

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported privately to the project maintainers.

Reports should include:

- A description of the behavior
- Relevant context or evidence
- Any additional information that may help with review

All reports will be handled respectfully and reviewed as fairly as possible.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all OSRI community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues and pull requests
- Discussions
- Community events
- Other official project channels
